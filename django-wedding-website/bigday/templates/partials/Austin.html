Austin Guide

If you are not from Austin and have some spare time before the wedding, we recommend you try some of the local attractions. Austin has a rich cultural history, with several well known restaurants, attractions, and parks. Below are some suggestions we have for you to try.


Food
Austin has a lot of local restaurants we highly recommend you try. The two cuisines Austin is most famous for are Bar-B-Que (BBQ) and tacos. For BBQ, Terry Black's is an excellent option (order beef fatty brisket), just be sure to get there 10 minutes before it opens. We do not recommend the famous Franklin's BBQ because of the long line. For tacos, Tacodeli has good breakfast tacos and Torchys Tacos has excellent street tacos for lunch.

A few other famous places are Hopdoddy for burgers (can be an hour wait), Uchi and Uchiko for high-end Asian-Fusion, the Oasis on Lake Travis (has very nice views of the sunset but can be an hour wait), and the Hey Cupcake food truck is popular for desert.

For good coffee, we recommend Cafe Medici and Houndstooth.

Attractions
Besides food, Austin also has some local attractions we recommend.

There are a few nice museums close to the University of Texas campus. The Bob Bullock Texas History museum details the history of Texas. There is the Harry Ransom Center (free admission) that has a Gutenberg Bible (the first book very printed) and the oldest photograph. The Blanton Mesuem of Art also has some nice art work.

Alamo Drafthouse is the local movie theatre and allows you to order dinner during your movie.

If you are interesting in authentic Texas Dancing, the Broken Spoke is a local Texas themed two-step dance bar with live country music.

Parks/Trails
There are several outdoor sites around the greater Austin area. Zilker park is a large park across the river from downtown Austin. Close by is Barton Springs, a natural pool with very cold water (below 68°F/20°C). Lady Bird Lake is also popular for stand-up-paddle boarding and canoeing.

For trails, many residents like to walk along Lady Bird Trail close to downtown Austin. There are also several "Green Belts" that offer shady hiking trails. For a nice view of the Colorado River, Mount Bonnell is an easy hike.
